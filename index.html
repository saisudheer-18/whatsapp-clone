Ma'am, if you ever visit Andhra Pradesh, please consider the *Sri Durga Malleswara Swamy Temple* (also known as **Kanaka Durga Temple**) in Vijayawada. 🛕 It's perched atop the Indrakeeladri hill beside the Krishna River — a self-manifested shrine to Goddess Kanaka Durga, revered since ancient times.
Legend says Sage Indrakeela did penance here, and the goddess appeared to slay Mahishasura, hence the name *Indrakeeladri*. It’s believed Goddess Durga is *Swayambhu*, deeply potent and alive in that sacred idol.
There are over **400 stone steps** leading to the top — each step climbed feels like a journey inward. On reaching, you see her eight-armed form, radiant with weapons and ornaments, standing over Mahishasura. The air hums with ancient energy.
During **Navratri/Dussehra**, the temple becomes a golden paradise — each day a different form of the goddess is worshipped: Swarna Kavacha, Annapurna, Saraswati, to name a few. The grand finale, Theppotsavam, is a majestic boat procession on the Krishna — truly a sea of devotion and light.
Pilgrims come in tens of thousands, lighting ghee lamps by the riverbanks, offering sarees at the goddess’s feet (especially during *Ashada Sare Utsavam*), chanting with tears of love — it's a celebration of faith that transforms you.
Standing there with the river flowing beneath, the city laid out beyond, and the divine presence in that sanctum — it stays with you long after. It's not just a visit; it's **a soulful pilgrimage**. 🌸✨
